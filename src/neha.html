Description

Dombivli is a city in the Thane of Maharashtra state in Konkan division, located about 40 km from Mumbai CST and is a part of Mumbai Metropolitan Region (MMR)

It is a fast-growing town and its vicinity to Mumbai and Thane and the availability of local train services from the place has given it the position of the suburb of Mumbai

The most frequently used mode of commute from Dombivli is the local train services and is one of the busiest railroad station

Highlight

The city is abound with numerous fairs and festivals throughout the year

Traditional, religious, state and national festivals are organized and celebrated in the city, depicting one or more aspects of human life, relationships or ancient traditions

The place is a pioneer for the celebrations of the festivals throughout the year

Geography

Dombivli is located at 19.218433°N 73.086718°E. It has an average elevation of 13.534 metres (44.403 feet)

The land here is rough and rocky by nature

Weather: 78°F (26°C), Wind N at 6 mph (10 km/h), 91% Humidity

The climate is warm and humid

History

Dombivli was first documented in the year 1075 by King Harpal Dev on stone inscriptions, situated at Mahul village near the Turbhe port

Some of the stone writings in Dombivli referring to its existence during the years 1396-97 confirms the fact

Dombivli Station was built earlier in 1887, which puts this in some of the oldest local railway station built
