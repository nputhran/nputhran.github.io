Description

Darbhanga is the fifth largest city in the state of Bihar.

A submetropolitan city of North Bihar and municipal corporation in Bihar, India, with a population of nearly 4 million people.

Highlight

Darbhanga is the biggest medical hub in Bihar.

The main languages spoken in this district are Maithili, Hindi, Urdu, however Hindi is used for official documentation.

It is also famous for Mithila Painting.

Geography

Darbhanga is located in the northern part of Bihar.

History

The history of Darbhanga dates back to Vedic periods. The name Darbhanga is the mutated form of "Dwarbanga".

That is, it is the combination of words "Dwar" (Gate) and "Banga" (Bengal) meaning "Gateway of Bengal". Some scholars say that Darbhanga was named after Dar (Dwar) and Bhangaa which means broken gates.

It is assumed that the gates of the Qila (at Qilaghat probably) were broken (by cannons or elephants) in 1326 AD when Tughlak forces attacked the last independent North Indian Hindu king.
