History

Han Dynasty

In 221, Sun Quan, a major warlord during the Three Kingdoms (AD 184/220-280) period moved the capital of Kingdom Eastern Wu to È, and renamed È to Wuchang (literally prospering from military, regarding its logistics role of the military bases established before the Battle of Red Cliffs.

Qin Empire

At the end of the Qing Empire (AD 1644-1912), the Wuchang Prefecture was the capital of the combined provinces of Hubei and Hunan, called the 'two Hu' or Huguang Viceroyalty. It was the seat of the provincial government of Huguang, at the head of which was a viceroy of Huguang. Next to Nanjing and Guangzhou, it was one of the most important vice-royalties in the empire.

Arrow War

During the Arrow War (AD 1856-1860), the government of the Qing dynasty was defeated by the western powers and signed the Convention of Peking, on the basis of which, an area of 11.8 square miles along the Yangtze River in Wuhan become a British Concession.

Wuchang Uprising

On October 10, 1911, Sun Yat-sen's followers launched the Wuchang Uprising, which led to the collapse of the Qing dynasty,as well as the establishment of the Republic of China. Wuhan was the capital of a leftist Kuomintang government led by Wang Jingwei, in opposition to Chiang Kai-shek and the nationalist government during the 1920s.

World War II

In early October 1938, Japanese troops occupied Wuhan. As a key center on the Yangtze, Wuhan was an important base for Japanese operations in China. On 18 December 1944, Wuhan was bombed by 77 American bombers that set off a firestorm that destroyed much of the city.

1950s

After the establishment of Communist People’s Republic of China, Wuhan was selected as one of the major cities to implement industrialization due to its strategic location. Wuhan Iron and Steel Factory, the first iron and steel factory established in PR China, and Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge, the first bridge on the Yangtze River were constructed during 1950s with the assitance from Soviet Union.

Geography

Wuhan is the most populous city in Central China, and being know as “Jiusheng Tongqu(the nine provinces leading thoroughfare)”. Because of it key role in domestic transportation and its vast urban lake system, it is sometimes referred to as “the Chicago of China” (Time, June 13, 1938).
Wuhan is located along both banks of the Yangtze River, the world's second longest river, with an area of 31.8 square miles and a population of 10.61 million(2015).
